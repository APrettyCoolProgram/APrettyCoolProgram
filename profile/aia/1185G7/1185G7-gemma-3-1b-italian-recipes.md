Okay, here are three incredibly easy Italian recipes you can whip up at home, perfect for a weeknight meal or weekend project:

**1. Simple Spaghetti with Tomato & Basil Sauce (Pasta Fresca)**

* **Yields:** 4 servings
* **Prep time:** 15 minutes
* **Cook time:** 20-30 minutes

**Ingredients:**

*   1 pound spaghetti
*   1 tbsp olive oil
*   2 cloves garlic, minced
*   1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
*   1/2 cup chopped fresh basil
*   Salt and pepper to taste
*   Pinch of sugar (optional - balances acidity)


**Instructions:**

1.  Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to package directions until al dente (firm but still slightly chewy). Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
2.  While the pasta is cooking, heat the olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds, until fragrant (don’t burn it!).
3.  Pour in the crushed tomatoes and bring to a simmer. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar (if using). Stir well.
4.  Add the cooked spaghetti to the sauce and toss to coat evenly. Add some reserved pasta water if the sauce is too thick – this helps it cling to the pasta.
5.  Stir in the chopped fresh basil. Serve immediately.

**2. One-Pan Lemon Herb Chicken & Roasted Vegetables (Caponata Style)**

* **Yields:** 4 servings
* **Prep time:** 10 minutes
* **Cook time:** 30-40 minutes


**Ingredients:**

*   1.5 - 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
*   1 large onion, chopped
*   2 cloves garlic, minced
*   2 lemons (zest and juice)
*   1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
*   1 lb baby potatoes, halved or quartered
*   4 sprigs fresh rosemary
*   2 tbsp olive oil
*   Salt and pepper to taste


**Instructions:**

1.  Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
2.  In a large bowl, toss the chicken with salt and pepper. Spread in a single layer on a baking sheet.
3.  In the same bowl, combine the chopped onion, garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, halved cherry tomatoes, potatoes, rosemary sprigs, olive oil, salt and pepper. Toss well to combine.
4.  Place the chicken and vegetable mixture in a 9x13 inch baking dish (or similar sized pan). Spread evenly.
5.  Bake for 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender. Let stand for 5 minutes before serving.

**3. Simple Sausage & Spinach Pasta Bake**

* **Yields:** 6 servings
* **Prep time:** 15 minutes
* **Cook time:** 20-25 minutes


**Ingredients:**

*   1 pound Italian sausage, removed from casings (sweet or hot)
*   8 ounces baby spinach, washed and dried
*   1 teaspoon salt
*   1/2 teaspoon black pepper
*   1 lb pasta of your choice (penne, rotini, or shells work well)
*   1 jar (24-ounce) marinara sauce
*   1 cup grated Parmesan cheese


**Instructions:**

1.  Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
2.  Heat the sausage in a large skillet over medium heat, breaking it up with a spoon. Drain off any excess grease.
3.  Add the spinach and salt and pepper to the skillet. Cook until the spinach wilts slightly, about 3-5 minutes.
4.  Meanwhile, cook pasta according to package directions until al dente.
5.  In a large bowl, combine the cooked pasta, sausage mixture, marinara sauce, and Parmesan cheese. Mix well to ensure everything is evenly coated.
6.  Pour into a greased 9x13 inch baking dish. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until bubbly and golden brown.


**Important Notes:**

*   **Fresh Ingredients are Key:** For the best flavor, use fresh herbs whenever possible.
*   **Seasoning is Everything:** Don't be shy with salt and pepper - they really bring out the flavors of Italian food!
*   **Adapt to Your Taste:** These recipes are starting points – feel free to adjust ingredients and seasonings to your liking.
